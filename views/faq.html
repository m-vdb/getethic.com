Frequently Asked Questions

Is ethic an insurance company?
No, Ethic is not. Ethic is a platform for mutualizing small claims. Large claims are still covered by your own insurer.
Is Ethic transparent?
Yes, every payment made by or to our members is handled in a transparent way. To achieve this, Ethic leverages cutting-edge technology called block chain.
What is an insurance deductible?
In auto insurance, the deductible is the amount the policyholder should pay himself before his insurer steps in. Typically, the higher the deductible, the lower the premium.
How does Ethic really lowers premiums without reducing coverage?
For auto coverage, the most common deductible in the United States is $500. If you chose a higher deductible, you would likely see a considerable drop in your premium, but you would now be exposed to paying this amount yourself. Ethic matches you to similar policyholders to cover anonymously this additional deductible. This is how you get lower premiums without carrying additional risk.
What do I have to pay on Ethic?
Every month, you will pay:
  • Your fair share of the total claimed within your pool in the past month.
  • An additional $2 service fee.
Can I lose money using Ethic?
No, Ethic is built in such a way that you cannot. There is a cap on the amount that you can be charged each month, and this cap ensures that you can’t lose money with Ethic.
How do I join Ethic?
  • After you register on the website, we first pull information from the DMV. We do this to make sure dangerous drivers do not get access to the platform and penalize you and the rest of the community.
  • You then call your insurance company (or agent) and ask to raise both your collision and comprehensive deductibles. When doing so, you will have the opportunity to estimate your savings. This change will be effective the same day.
  • Once on the platform, behind the scenes, we attribute you to a large pool of members with a similar profile to yours. To preserve privacy, this pool is fully anonymous.
What does Ethic cover?
For auto insurance, Ethic covers any damage subject to a deductible:
  • Damage resulting from an at-fault collision
  • Damage resulting from what is typically referred to as "comprehensive"
What does Ethic not cover?
For auto insurance, Ethic does not cover:
  • Hospital expenses
  • Damage that was not your fault, which is covered in full by the other driver’s insurer
In case of a claim, what is my out-of-pocket amount?
When you file a claim, you will be required to pay the first $500, including the $20 commission for handling the claim. This out-of-pocket is meant to avoid fraud and abusive claims, which would harm our members.
Is it in Ethic’s interest to deny claims or be conservative on payouts?
No. Claims are paid directly by Ethic members, not Ethic itself. This is a paradigm shift versus the traditional insurance industry. Ethic’s role is to make sure payouts are fair to everyone.
Who is in charge of handling claims?
Ethic applies the “sharing economy” principle to claim auditing. Instead of speaking with your insurer’s employee, Ethic trains independent partners to autonomously lead you through the claim process, damage estimate and final payout. These independent partners have no incentive against you and are paid a flat amount for every claim they handle.
OK, I got into an accident that I am responsible for. What do I do next?
You first file your claim in the Ethic app or website. You will be asked to provide a few photos and any additional document you may have. One of our partners will get in touch with you, listen to your story and guide you through the process. Depending on the level of damage, he will inform you whether you should file a claim with your own insurer to receive additional coverage.
OK, I got into an accident that i am NOT responsible for. What do I do next?
The accident is not your fault, hence the other driver’s insurer must pay for the damage their policyholder inflicted to your vehicle. You must file a claim with the other driver’s insurer and quickly notify us using the Ethic app or website.
The other driver’s insurer refuses to pay for the damage. What should I do?
Unfortunately, some insurance companies do everything they can to avoid paying out claims to claimants. It happens. In this case, you must file a claim with your own insurer and pay your deductible. To make things easy for you, after making sure you tried to get paid by the other driver’s insurer, Ethic will pay this deductible for you, in full. In most cases, your insurer will go after the other driver’s insurer and is legally obliged to get you your deductible back, or at least part of it. When this occurs, you will be asked to pay the Ethic community back.
Does Ethic work with my current insurer?
Ethic is designed to integrate with as many personal auto insurance policies as possible. Here is a list of insurance companies Ethic can be used with. If your current carrier does not appear in this list, please let us know.
How does Ethic promote honesty and trust?
Ethic is a platform on which members cover each other directly. Being fair to other members means keeping claims low and reasonable. Don’t forget, Ethic is a community of people who rely on each other’s honesty.
